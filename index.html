Snake AI

Utilizing Python and PyTorch, I developed a QLearning AI program to play the classic game, Snake. Through 2,000 generations, the AI achieved a high score of 94 and an average score of 31 with a steady increase overtime.

Automated Twitter Account

Using Python and the Twitter API, Tweepy, I developed an automated Twitter account that tweeted out as if it was the Spartan Statue at the Michigan State University campus. It tweets out the weather daily in East Lansing, MI, as well as when sporting events are for football, basketball, wrestling, etc.

LocoListen

Using streamlit, a python website framework, and SQLite, I worked in a team of 4 during the Michigan State Hackathon to develop a way to connect people with music and location. Users would upload songs and playlists they wanted to share and these would be recommended to other users based on location. Locally hosted for the hackathon, Gitub can be found below.

Ford 1896

As a part of a high school CTSO, I worked in a team of two to create a video game, using p5.js, that tells the story of Henry Ford and the Ford Motor Company. This project went on to win 2nd place at the National Leadership Conference for Business Professionals of America.

Recipe Web Scraper

Using Selenium, I created a webscraper that took recipes from the website pickuplimes.com and wrote them to a text file with all the relevant recipe information such as ingredients, portion size, directions, etc.

Calculus Ninja

During high school, I created a video game that taught basic concepts of Calculus for a project in my AP Calculus class. Players must dodge obstacles while figuring out simple calculus problems in order to become the Calculus Ninja.
